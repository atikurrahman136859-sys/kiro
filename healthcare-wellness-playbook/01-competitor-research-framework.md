# 01 · Competitor Research Framework

A repeatable method to audit competitors in healthcare & wellness, find gaps, and define
the client's "wedge." Output feeds directly into the positioning & strategy doc (02).

---

## Step 1 — Build the competitor set

Identify **8–12** competitors across three tiers:

- **Direct:** same service + same geography/audience (the ones who steal the booking).
- **Indirect:** solve the same problem differently (e.g., a clinic vs. a wellness app).
- **Aspirational:** best-in-class brands (often national) to benchmark quality/UX.

How to find them:
- Google the client's top service + location ("invisalign austin", "physiotherapy near me")
- Google Maps / local pack for local healthcare
- Review sites (Healthgrades, Zocdoc, Yelp, Trustpilot, Google Reviews)
- Meta Ad Library & Google Ads Transparency Center (who's actively advertising)
- Industry directories and insurer provider lists

---

## Step 2 — Score each competitor (use the matrix)

For each competitor, capture:

| Dimension | What to record |
|-----------|----------------|
| Positioning | Their core promise / tagline / who they target |
| Services & offers | Service list, packages, free entry offers, pricing visibility |
| Messaging & tone | Clinical vs. warm, fear-based vs. empowering, proof used |
| Conversion path | Primary CTA, booking flow, # of steps, friction points |
| Trust signals | Credentials, reviews count/rating, affiliations, media, awards |
| Content/SEO | Topics they rank for, blog depth, location pages, FAQs |
| Paid presence | Running ads? Channels? Angles/offers in ads? |
| UX/Design | Speed, mobile, accessibility, visual quality (1–5) |
| Compliance posture | Privacy/cookie handling, disclaimers, testimonial use |

> Use `competitor-matrix.csv` (below) to score consistently.

---

## Step 3 — Page-by-page teardown (top 3 direct competitors)

For the homepage + a key service page + the booking flow, note:
- **Above the fold:** headline, subhead, primary CTA, hero proof
- **Trust stack:** reviews, credentials, guarantees, insurance/badges
- **Objection handling:** FAQs, pricing transparency, "what to expect"
- **Conversion mechanics:** click-to-call, online booking, forms, chat
- **Speed & mobile:** run PageSpeed Insights; note Core Web Vitals
- **What annoyed you as a user?** (friction = your client's opportunity)

---

## Step 4 — SEO & content gap analysis

- Pull competitor keywords (Ahrefs/SEMrush/Ubersuggest) — what drives their traffic?
- Identify **content gaps**: high-intent topics no competitor covers well.
- Identify **local SEO gaps**: missing/weak location pages, GBP optimization, reviews.
- Note backlink sources (medical directories, local press, partnerships) to replicate.

---

## Step 5 — Synthesize into opportunities

Summarize findings as:

1. **What everyone does (table stakes):** must-haves you can't skip.
2. **What most do poorly (the wedge):** where your client can clearly win.
3. **What nobody does (differentiation):** white-space to own.
4. **Positioning recommendation:** one sentence on how the client should stand apart.
