# 02 · Positioning & Conversion Strategy

Turn research (01) into a clear position in the market and a conversion engine — while
staying inside healthcare/wellness legal and ethical limits.

---

## Part A — Positioning

### A1. Unique Value Proposition (UVP)
Fill the formula:

> For **[target patient/customer]** who **[problem/need]**, **[brand]** is the
> **[category]** that **[key differentiator]** — unlike **[alternative]**, we
> **[proof of difference]**.

Example (dental):
> For busy families who dread the dentist, Brightsmile is the family dental practice that
> makes visits genuinely calm and on-time — unlike rushed chain clinics, we guarantee
> same-day emergency slots and a no-judgment promise.

### A2. Messaging hierarchy
1. **Primary promise** (the one thing — appears in the hero)
2. **Proof pillars** (3–4 reasons to believe: credentials, outcomes, experience, reviews)
3. **Differentiators** (what only you can claim)
4. **Tone of voice** (warm + credible is the winning blend in health; avoid fear-mongering)

### A3. Trust positioning (non-negotiable in health)
Health buyers choose with **trust first, features second**. Lead with:
- Credentials & qualifications (named, verifiable)
- Real outcomes / reviews (compliant with profession rules — see doc 04)
- Safety, hygiene, privacy assurances
- Human warmth (real photos of real staff > stock images)

## Part B — The Offer

### B1. Offer ladder
Design a low-risk entry that converts cold visitors, then ascend:

```
Free / Low-risk entry → Core service → Recurring / membership → Premium
(e.g., free consult)    (treatment)     (maintenance plan)       (full program)
```

- **Lead magnet / entry offer:** free consultation, symptom checker, assessment guide,
  first-visit offer (must comply with anti-inducement rules in some jurisdictions).
- **Core offer:** the primary service the site should book.
- **Risk reversal:** satisfaction promise, transparent pricing, easy rescheduling,
  insurance help. (Never guarantee medical *outcomes* — that's non-compliant.)

### B2. Pricing presentation
- Transparency builds trust; show ranges or "starting from" where possible.
- Explain insurance/financing clearly — a top objection in healthcare.

## Part C — Conversion Strategy

### C1. Define the conversion goal & funnel
Primary macro-conversion (pick one): **book appointment / request call / buy / sign up**.

Map the journey and the job of each stage:

| Stage | Visitor mindset | Page/asset | Micro-conversion |
|-------|-----------------|-----------|------------------|
| Awareness | "Is this for me?" | Blog, service page, ads | Read / scroll / click |
| Consideration | "Can I trust them?" | Service detail, reviews, bios, FAQ | Save, compare, watch video |
| Decision | "Book or not?" | Booking page, contact, pricing | **Book / call / form** |
| Post-conversion | "Did I choose well?" | Confirmation, prep email, reminders | Show up / refer |

### C2. Conversion principles for healthcare/wellness
- **One primary CTA per page**, repeated (sticky header "Book", click-to-call on mobile).
- **Reduce friction:** online booking beats forms; minimize fields; offer call + book + chat.
- **Mobile-first:** most local-health searches are mobile and high-intent.
- **Speed = conversions:** every second of load time costs bookings.
- **Reassure at the point of action:** privacy note near forms ("Your info is confidential").
- **Local trust:** show address, map, parking, hours, insurance accepted.

### C3. Ethical persuasion (within limits)
Use these — responsibly:
- **Social proof:** review counts, ratings, testimonials (where allowed), patient stories.
- **Authority:** credentials, affiliations, media mentions, research.
- **Scarcity/urgency:** *only when true* (e.g., "limited new-patient slots this month").
  Never fabricate urgency around health — it's unethical and often non-compliant.
- **Risk reversal:** transparent process, easy cancellation, money-back on non-clinical items.

> ⚠️ Do **not** use: fear-based manipulation, guaranteed cures/outcomes, fake countdown
> timers, or unverifiable claims. The FTC requires health claims to be truthful and
> substantiated. See doc 04.

### C4. Conversion targets (set baselines)
- Booking/lead conversion rate target (typical local-health range: 3–8% of sessions)
- Call rate, form completion rate, booking-flow drop-off
- Lead-to-appointment and appointment-to-show rates (ops matter as much as the site)
