# 07 · Growth, CRO & Analytics

How to measure, optimize, and scale after launch — the difference between a site that
"looks nice" and one that compounds bookings/revenue over time.

---

## 1. Measurement framework (set up before launch)

### North Star + KPI tree
- **North Star:** the #1 business outcome (e.g., booked appointments/month).
- **Primary KPIs:** conversion rate, leads/bookings, cost per acquisition (CPA),
  appointment show rate, revenue / lifetime value (LTV).
- **Supporting KPIs:** traffic by channel, bounce/engagement, booking-flow drop-off,
  call volume, email open/click, review rating/volume, page speed (CWV).

### Tracking stack (compliance-aware)
- **GA4 + Google Tag Manager**, **Google Search Console**, **call tracking**,
  booking/CRM analytics, **Microsoft Clarity / Hotjar** (heatmaps + session replay).
- Define **conversion events:** book_appointment, call_click, form_submit, get_directions,
  add_to_cart/purchase (wellness e-comm), newsletter_signup.
- **PHI guardrails:** do NOT send PHI/health data to analytics or ad platforms; mask
  sensitive fields; consider server-side tagging; gate trackers behind consent (doc 04).

## 2. Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO)

### The loop
```
Measure → Find leaks → Hypothesize → Prioritize → Test → Learn → Repeat
```

### Find the leaks
- Funnel analysis: where do users drop (service page → booking → confirmation)?
- Heatmaps/scroll maps: what gets ignored? Where do clicks die?
- Session replays: watch real friction (form errors, confusion, mobile issues).
- Voice of customer: post-booking micro-surveys ("what almost stopped you?").

### Common healthcare/wellness CRO wins
- Add/strengthen online booking; reduce form fields; add click-to-call on mobile.
- Surface trust earlier (reviews, credentials, insurance accepted) above the fold.
- Add "what to expect" + pricing/insurance clarity to reduce anxiety/objections.
- Faster pages; clearer single CTA; sticky booking button.
- Better local content + GBP for higher-intent local traffic.

### Prioritize tests
Use **ICE/PIE** scoring (Impact, Confidence, Ease). Test highest-leverage first.

### Testing method
- A/B test with enough traffic for significance; one primary metric per test.
- For low-traffic local sites, favor **best-practice redesigns + qualitative evidence**
  over slow A/B tests; validate with before/after + funnel metrics.
- Document every test: hypothesis, result, decision, learning.

## 3. Retention & lifecycle growth
- **Recall/recare flows** (dental cleanings, check-ups, refills) — major LTV driver.
- Post-visit follow-up + review requests (compliant).
- Newsletters with genuinely useful health content (not just promos).
- Membership/loyalty/wellness plans for recurring revenue.
- Reactivation campaigns for lapsed patients/clients.

---

## 4. Scaling what works
- Double down on channels/pages with proven ROI; cut what doesn't.
- Expand winning content clusters; add location/service pages.
- Increase paid budget only after CPA + show-rate are validated.
- Productize repeatable wins into the playbook for the next client.

---

## 5. Reporting cadence

| Cadence | Audience | Contents |
|---------|----------|----------|
| Weekly (internal) | Agency team | Pacing, anomalies, test status, ad health |
| Monthly | Client | KPIs vs. targets, wins, CRO results, next actions |
| Quarterly | Client leadership | Strategy review, ROI, roadmap, budget reallocation |

**Reporting principles:** tie every metric to the business outcome (bookings/revenue),
show trends not vanity numbers, always end with "what we'll do next."
